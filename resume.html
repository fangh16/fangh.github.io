Programmable Confinement for Structural Control

External fields, spatial constraints, and geometry can reshape the energy landscapes of interfacial colloidal systems. Building on various forms of geometric constraints, this direction explores how programmable confinement stabilizes complex lattices, drives polymorphic transitions, and creates switchable structures with tunable functional responses.

Non-ergodic Dynamics in Self-assembly

Self-assembly pathways often do not simply relax toward equilibrium states. In soft materials, impurities, curvature, and directed external driving can introduce kinetic frustration and pathway bifurcation, thereby selecting final morphologies through non-ergodic dynamics.

Data-driven Strategies for Inverse Design

Experiments, simulations, theory, and machine learning can be integrated into a data-generation and design platform. This direction aims to predict morphology and dynamical pathways, identify interpretable descriptors from high-dimensional structural data, and use target structures or responses to infer the required geometry, field protocol, composition, or driving condition.
