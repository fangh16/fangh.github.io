Published Work

Selected publications with representative images and short summaries. † indicates equal contribution; * indicates corresponding author.

Nature 652, 635-642 (2026)

Dual-symmetry-guided assembly of complex lattices

Huang Fang†, Xiaotian Li†, Wensi Sun†, Chengxin Wang, Nuo Chen, Yining Gan, Jiping Huang, Yuqiang Ma*, Hajime Tanaka*, Peng Tan*

This work uses dual-symmetry principles and programmable confinement to guide the assembly of complex two-dimensional lattices, offering a route to structures that are difficult to obtain through conventional thermodynamic self-assembly.

Nature Physics 21, 938-946 (2025)

Impact of impurities on crystal growth

Qiong Gao†, Huang Fang†, Dong Xiang, Yanshuang Chen, Hajime Tanaka*, Peng Tan*

The study reveals how impurities reshape crystal growth beyond the dilute-perturbation picture, producing kinetic frustration, interface instability, and morphology selection during ordering.

Nature Communications 16, 2819 (2025)

Dynamic and asymmetric colloidal molecules

Huang Fang†, Qiong Gao†, Yujie Rong†, Yanshuang Chen, Jiping Huang, Hua Tong, Zhihong Nie*, Hajime Tanaka*, Wei Li*, Peng Tan*

This paper explores dynamically reconfigurable colloidal clusters with asymmetric structures, connecting particle-level interactions to emergent soft-matter molecular behavior.

Physical Review Letters 132, 018202 (2024)

Phase Reentrances and Solid Deformations in Confined Colloidal Crystals

Xiaoxia Li†, Huang Fang†, Krongtum Sankaewtong†, Minhuan Li, Yanshuang Chen, Jiping Huang, Ran Ni*, Hajime Tanaka*, Peng Tan*

This study examines how confinement creates unusual reentrant phase behavior and solid deformations, illustrating how boundaries can change the rules of colloidal crystallization.

PNAS 117, 27927-27933 (2020)

Two-step crystallization and solid-solid transitions in binary colloidal mixtures

Huang Fang, Michael F. Hagan, W. Benjamin Rogers*

By combining experiments and modeling, this work shows how binary colloidal systems can crystallize through intermediate phases and solid-solid transformations rather than through a single direct pathway.
